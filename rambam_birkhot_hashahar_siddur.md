# <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בִּרְכוֹת הַשַּׁחַר לְפִי הָרַמְבַּ״ם</span>
A simple procedural siddur for the morning blessings

## Chapter 1: Birkhot Hashaḥar

> Say each berakha when its action happens.
>
> If the action did not happen, do not say that berakha.
>
> This chapter follows the Rambam’s procedural order for the morning blessings.

## Section 1: Waking and rising

### 1. Upon waking, while still in bed

This blessing belongs at waking, before getting out of bed.[^p1]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">אֱלֹהַי, נְשָׁמָה שֶׁנָּתַתָּ בִּי טְהוֹרָה, אַתָּה בְרָאתָהּ, אַתָּה יְצַרְתָּהּ, אַתָּה נְפַחְתָּהּ בִּי; וְאַתָּה מְשַׁמְרָהּ בְּקִרְבִּי, וְאַתָּה עָתִיד לִיטְּלָהּ מִמֶּנִּי וְאַתָּה עָתִיד לְהַחֲזִירָהּ בִּי לֶעָתִיד לָבוֹא.</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">כָּל זְמַן שֶׁהַנְּשָׁמָה תְּלוּיָה בְּקִרְבִּי, מוֹדֶה אֲנִי לְפָנֶיךָ יְיָ אֱלֹהַי רִבּוֹן כָּל הַמַּעֲשִׂים.</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, הַמַּחֲזִיר נְשָׁמוֹת לִפְגָרִים מֵתִים.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Elohai, neshamah shenatata bi tehorah, ata beratah, ata yetzarta, ata nefakhta bi; veata meshamrah bekirbi, veata atid litlah mimeni veata atid lehachzirah bi leatid lavo.
>
> Kol zeman shehaneshamah teluyah bekirbi, modeh ani lefanekha Adonai Elohai ribon kol hamaʿasim.
>
> Barukh ata Adonai, hamaḥazir neshamot lifgarim metim.

**Meaning:** The soul You placed in me is pure, and You restore souls to dead bodies.

### 2. Hearing the rooster or the daybreak signal

This blessing belongs only when the rooster or daybreak signal is actually relevant.[^p2]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם הַנוֹתֵן לַשֶׂכְוִי בִינָה לְהַבְחִין בֵּין יוֹם וּבֵין לָיְלָה.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam hanoten lasekhvi bina lehavḥin bein yom uvein laila.

**Meaning:** Who gives the rooster understanding to distinguish day from night.

### 3. When putting on clothing

This blessing belongs when dressing.[^p3]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם מַלְבִּישׁ עֲרוּמִים.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam malbish arumim.

**Meaning:** Who clothes the naked.

### 4. When covering the head

This blessing belongs when covering the head.[^p4]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם עוֹטֵר יִשְׂרָאֵל בְּתִפְאָרָה.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam oter Yisrael betifara.

**Meaning:** Who crowns Israel with glory.

### 5. When passing the hands over the eyes

This blessing belongs when the eyes first clear after sleep.[^p5]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">פּוֹקֵחַ עִוְרִים.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Poqeaḥ ivrim.

**Meaning:** Who opens the eyes of the blind.

### 6. When sitting up

This blessing belongs when sitting up.[^p6]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">מַתִּיר אֲסוּרִים.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Matir asurim.

**Meaning:** Who frees the bound.

### 7. When lowering the feet to the ground

This blessing belongs when the feet touch the floor.[^p7]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">רוֹקַע הָאָרֶץ עַל הַמָּיִם.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Roqea haaretz al hamayim.

**Meaning:** Who spreads the earth over the waters.

### 8. When standing upright

This blessing belongs when standing upright.[^p8]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">זוֹקֵף כְּפוּפִים.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Zoqef kefufim.

**Meaning:** Who straightens the bowed.

## Section 2: Cleansing and dressing

### 9. When washing hands

This blessing belongs when washing the hands.[^p9]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל נְטִילַת יָדָיִם.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam asher qiddeshanu bemitzvotav vetzivanu al netilat yadayim.

**Meaning:** Who sanctified us by His commandments and commanded us concerning washing hands.

### 10. When washing the face

This blessing belongs with washing the face, as the morning sleep clears.[^p10]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם הַמַּעֲבִיר חֶבְלֵי שֵׁינָה מֵעֵינַי וּתְנוּמָה מֵעַפְעַפָּי;</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ יְיָ אֱלֹהַי וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתַי שֶׁתַּרְגִּילֵנִי לִדְבַר מִצְוָה וְאַל תַּרְגִּילֵנִי לִדְבַר עֲבֵרָה וְעָוֹן.</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">וְתִשְׁלֹט בִּי יֵצֶר טוֹב וְאַל יִשְׁלֹט בִּי יֵצֶר רָע. וּתְחַזְּקֵנִי בְּמִצְוֹתֶיךָ וְתֵן חֶלְקִי בְּתוֹרָתֶךָ.</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">וְתִתְּנֵנִי לְחֵן וּלְחֶסֶד וּלְרַחֲמִים בְּעֵינֶיךָ וּבְעֵינֵי כָּל רוֹאַי וְתִגְמְלֵנִי חֲסָדִים טוֹבִים. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ גּוֹמֵל חֲסָדִים טוֹבִים.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam hamaʿavir ḥevlei shena meʿeinai utnuma meʿafapai;
>
> yehi ratzon milfanekha Adonai Elohai veElohei avotai shetargileni lidvar mitzvah veal targileni lidvar aveirah veavon.
>
> Vetishloṭ bi yetzer tov veal yishloṭ bi yetzer ra. Uteḥazqeni bemitzvotekha veten ḥelqi betoratekha.
>
> Vetitneni leḥen uleḥesed uleraḥamim beʿeinekha uveʿeinei kol roʿai vetigmeleni ḥasadim tovim. Barukh ata Adonai gomel ḥasadim tovim.

**Meaning:** Who removes sleep from my eyes and asks that I be led to mitzvot and kindness.

### 11. After using the bathroom

This blessing belongs after leaving the bathroom.[^p11]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אֲשֶׁר יָצַר אֶת הָאָדָם בְּחָכְמָה וּבָרָא בוֹ נְקָבִים נְקָבִים חֲלוּלִים חֲלוּלִים;</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">גָּלוּי וְיָדוּעַ לִפְנֵי כִּסֵּא כְבוֹדֶךָ שֶׁאִם יִסָּתֵם אֶחָד מֵהֶם אוֹ אִם יִפָּתֵחַ אֶחָד מֵהֶם אִי אֶפְשָׁר לְהִתְקַיֵּם אֲפִלּוּ שָׁעָה אֶחָת.</span>
>
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ רוֹפֵא כָּל בָּשָׂר וּמַפְלִיא לַעֲשׂוֹת.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam asher yatzar et haadam beḥokhma uvara bo neqavim neqavim ḥalulim ḥalulim;
>
> galui veyadua lifnei kise kevodekha sheim yistam eḥad meihem o im yipataḥ eḥad meihem i efshar lehitqayem afilu shaah eḥat.
>
> Barukh ata Adonai rofeh kol basar umafli laʿasot.

**Meaning:** The human body is formed with wisdom and cannot function without its openings.

### 12. When fastening the belt

This blessing belongs when fastening the belt.[^p12]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אוֹזֵר יִשְׂרָאֵל בִּגְבוּרָה.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam ozer Yisrael bigvura.

**Meaning:** Who girds Israel with strength.

### 13. When putting on shoes

This blessing belongs when putting on shoes.[^p13]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם שֶׁעָשִׂיתָ לִי כָּל צָרְכִּי.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam sheasita li kol tzorkhi.

**Meaning:** For providing all my needs.

### 14. When beginning to walk

This blessing belongs when beginning to walk.[^p14]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם הַמֵּכִין מִצְעֲדֵי גָּבֶר.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam hamekhin mitzadei gaver.

**Meaning:** Who prepares a man’s steps.

## Section 3: Daily identity

### 15. Daily identity berakha

This daily identity blessing is said every day.[^p15]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם שֶׁלֹּא עָשַׂנִי גּוֹי.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam shelo asani goy.

**Meaning:** Who did not make me a non-Jew.

### 16. Daily identity berakha

This daily identity blessing is said every day.[^p16]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם שֶׁלֹּא עָשַׂנִי אִשָּׁה.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam shelo asani isha.

**Meaning:** Who did not make me a woman.

### 17. Daily identity berakha

This daily identity blessing is said every day.[^p17]

**Hebrew:**
> <span dir="rtl" lang="he">בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם שֶׁלֹּא עָשַׂנִי עָבֶד.</span>

**Transliteration:**
> Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh haolam shelo asani aved.

**Meaning:** Who did not make me a servant.
